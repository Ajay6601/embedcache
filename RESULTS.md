# embedcache: with vs without, consolidated real measurements

This page puts the **with-embedcache** and **without-embedcache** numbers side by side for
every scenario we have measured. It is a hand-curated summary; every figure is copied from a
report that is **regenerated by code** in this repo against **real backends and real data**:
follow the source link to see how each one is produced and to re-run it yourself. Nothing here
is projected or hand-tuned.

"Without" means the baseline the report actually measured: straight to the backend, a cold
cache, fixed-size chunking, or every request recomputed, whichever is the honest comparison
for that scenario.

---

## 1. Does adding embedcache cost you anything? (quality & correctness)

The most important comparison: turning embedcache **on** must not change your results. It
doesn't.

| Measurement | Without embedcache | With embedcache | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| BEIR SciFact nDCG@10 (retrieval quality) | 0.5221 | 0.5221 (cold **and** warm) | [BENCHMARKS.md](BENCHMARKS.md) |
| BEIR SciFact Recall@100 | 0.8297 | 0.8297 (cold **and** warm) | [BENCHMARKS.md](BENCHMARKS.md) |
| Top-10 ranking changes across 300 real queries | (baseline) | **0 queries differ** | [BENCHMARKS.md](BENCHMARKS.md) |
| Byte-exact response replay | ground truth | identical bytes on **6/6 models** | [VALIDATION.md](VALIDATION.md) |
| Randomized mixed-batch correctness | ground truth | 3,453 embeddings, **0 mismatches** | [EXPERIMENTS.md](EXPERIMENTS.md) |
| Correctness after a 429,968-request storm | ground truth | **500/500 byte-exact** | [PRODSIM.md](PRODSIM.md) |
| Survives a hard crash / restart | (baseline) | **500/500 byte-exact hits** from a 251 MB snapshot | [PRODSIM.md](PRODSIM.md) |

**Takeaway:** the retrieval-quality metrics are *bit-identical* on a recognized IR benchmark.
Adding embedcache is a transparent optimization, not a lossy approximation, so the only open
question is how much it saves.

## 2. How much it saves (upstream work: without vs with)

"Saved" = work that reached the backend **without** the cache but did **not** with it.

| Scenario (all real data) | Without embedcache | With embedcache | Saved | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Real RAG agent, 103 live Wikipedia articles, 8,601 chunks, zero constructed dupes | 1,519,377 tokens billed | 763,733 tokens billed | **755,644 tokens (49.7%)** | [REALTEST.md](REALTEST.md) |
| Nightly re-ingest, same corpus, nothing actually changed | re-embed 8,515 chunks (2m40s) | re-embed 0 chunks (4s) | **100%** absorbed | [REALTEST.md](REALTEST.md) |
| Re-ingest after editing ~5% of a real corpus (per model, 6 models) | re-embed 120 chunks | re-embed 6 chunks | **95.0%** absorbed | [VALIDATION.md](VALIDATION.md) |
| Re-ingest at scale, 50k-chunk corpus, 5% edited | recompute all | recompute only edited | **95.0%** absorbed | [PRODSIM.md](PRODSIM.md) |
| Re-ingest after a one-sentence edit (**chunk-diff vs fixed-size**) | 28.6% hit (fixed-size) | **93.8%** hit (content-defined) | 3.3× better | [CHUNKDIFF.md](CHUNKDIFF.md) |
| Search-shaped (Zipf) query traffic on a real corpus | every query recomputed | **~80%** hit rate (6 models) | ~80% | [VALIDATION.md](VALIDATION.md) |
| Agentic query-expansion loops | every variant recomputed | **79.2%** served from cache | 79.2% | [VALIDATION.md](VALIDATION.md) |
| Re-running a full eval suite (300 queries × 5,183 docs) | full first-pass embedding cost | **~2s, 100% absorbed** | 100% | [BENCHMARKS.md](BENCHMARKS.md) |

**Takeaway:** on workloads that contain repeats (RAG, re-ingest, eval loops, hot queries)
embedcache absorbs roughly **half to nearly all** of the upstream embedding work, at zero
quality cost. On genuinely novel, never-repeated traffic it saves nothing (exact-match only),
which is why `embedcache analyze` measures your real duplicate share before you install.

## 3. Latency: without (backend) vs with (cache hit)

Open-loop (constant-rate) measurement, so tail latency is honest; see [PERF.md](PERF.md).

| Model (dims) | Miss p50 (real compute) | Direct-to-backend p50 (no proxy) | **Hit p50** | Hit p99.9 | Proxy overhead on a miss |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| all-minilm (384) | 34.2 ms | 34.6 ms | **~0.0 ms** | 2.4 ms | −0.44 ms |
| nomic-embed-text (768) | 47.2 ms | 46.2 ms | **~0.0 ms** | 2.9 ms | +1.08 ms |
| mxbai-embed-large (1024) | 79.6 ms | 79.1 ms | **~0.0 ms** | 2.8 ms | +0.50 ms |

**Takeaway:** a cache hit is effectively free (sub-millisecond p50, ≤2.9 ms at p99.9), and on a
miss the proxy's own cost is within measurement noise (−0.44 to +1.08 ms, the miss path even
measured *faster* than direct on one model) against the backend's own tens of milliseconds. You
do not trade latency for the savings.

## 5. Multi-tenant & multi-agent behavior (without vs with enforcement)

| Behavior | Without embedcache | With embedcache | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| A runaway key exhausts its budget | keeps spending on the backend | **429** on new compute, cache hits keep serving | [VALIDATION.md](VALIDATION.md), [MULTIAGENT.md](MULTIAGENT.md) |
| Two agents embed the same query at once | two upstream calls | **1 call** (in-flight coalescing) | [MULTIAGENT.md](MULTIAGENT.md) |
| 200 concurrent identical requests | 200 upstream calls | **1 upstream call** | [EXPERIMENTS.md](EXPERIMENTS.md) |
| A Python/LangChain service reuses a Go agent's embedding | recomputed | **cache hit** (shared across languages) | [MULTIAGENT.md](MULTIAGENT.md) |
| Hostile/unauthorized traffic | reaches backend | **7,561/7,561 rejected 401** | [PRODSIM.md](PRODSIM.md) |

**Takeaway:** across independent callers, agents, and languages, embedcache turns duplicate and
concurrent work into a single upstream call and gives you a hard per-key spend cap, the
cost-control half of the story, not just the cache.

## Honest boundaries (measured, not hidden)

- **Exact-match by default.** A paraphrase does not hit unless you opt into semantic
  (near-duplicate) caching, which is off by default and shadow-first: it measures the real
  cosine gap between a near-neighbor and the truth before you ever serve an approximate vector
  ([SEMANTIC.md](SEMANTIC.md) measured mean 0.9824, worst 0.9386 at threshold 0.8, i.e. enough
  drift to keep gating carefully). With exact-match alone, savings are capped at your literal
  duplicate share.
- **Per-instance by default, shared on request.** A single instance keeps its cache in memory;
  point replicas at one Redis with `-shared-redis` and they share entries, so the fleet does not
  recompute the same vectors ([SHAREDCACHE.md](SHAREDCACHE.md)).
- **Token estimator on non-Latin scripts.** The estimator now counts CJK/wide runes per
  character rather than per byte; it still only feeds the *analyzer's* dollar estimate, never
  billing (billed spend is apportioned from the backend's exact reported total).
- **NFC vs NFD Unicode** folds with `-normalize nfc` (common Latin combining sequences, a
  zero-dependency subset of full Unicode NFC).
- **Multimodal images** are architecturally supported (content-agnostic fingerprint proven) but
  not yet live-tested end-to-end.
